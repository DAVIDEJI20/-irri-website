About IRRI

Where Others See Barriers, We Build Bridges

Across Nigeria, armed conflict, terrorism, and natural disasters leave thousands with newly acquired disabilities. For most, support ends when the emergency does.

People living with physical, sensory, and psychosocial disabilities face compounded barriers: disrupted services, limited healthcare, legal gaps, stigma, and exclusion from economic life. They are overlooked not because they lack potential, but because systems were not built with them in mind.

"We see a Nigeria where no one is defined by what happened to them."

Our Values

What We Stand For

Seven commitments that shape how we work, who we hire, and what we measure.

01

Dignity & Rights

We uphold the inherent dignity of every person with a disability, guided by international human rights standards.

02

Nothing About Us Without Us

People with lived experience of disability and conflict lead our decisions, programs, and governance.

03

Inclusion in Action

Accessibility and participation are built into everything we do: programs, partnerships, and workplaces.

04

Do No Harm

We operate with conflict sensitivity, trauma-informed care, strong safeguarding, and protection-first principles.

05

Accountability & Integrity

We steward resources transparently, measure what matters, and are accountable to communities and donors.

06

Collaboration Over Silos

We work across humanitarian, development, security, and private-sector partners to deliver joined-up solutions.

07

Local Leadership

We prioritize Nigerian leadership, build local capacity, and design for scale and sustainability.

Pilot Project

Inclusive Rehab-to-Work Pathway

A 3-month pilot in Abuja bringing rehabilitation, mental health support, skills training, and legal guidance into one clear pathway for 40 people.

Month 1

Intake & Rehabilitation

  • Community outreach with welfare units and local leaders
  • Functional assessments and individualized rehab plans
  • Protection screening and safeguarding setup
Month 2

Skills Sprint & Support

  • Skills tracks: phone repair, IT, tailoring, agribusiness
  • Legal and benefits navigation clinics
  • Ongoing psychosocial counselling
Month 3

Placement & Reintegration

  • Starter toolkits and micro-grants for 20 participants
  • Employer referrals and market linkage
  • Peer support groups and endline assessment
