About IRRI
Where Others See Barriers, We Build Bridges

Across Nigeria, armed conflict, terrorism, and natural disasters leave thousands of people with newly acquired disabilities. For most, the support system stops when the emergency ends.

People living with physical, sensory, and psychosocial disabilities face compounded barriers: disrupted services, limited access to healthcare and assistive devices, legal gaps, stigma, and exclusion from economic life. They are overlooked not because they lack potential, but because systems were not built with them in mind.

"We see a Nigeria where no one is defined by what happened to them. Where people who acquire disabilities through conflict or disaster are fully supported to rebuild their lives, on their own terms, in their own communities."

People in our programs gain access to rehabilitation, mental health support, and sustainable livelihood pathways. Everything we do is led by persons with disabilities and grounded in rights, evidence, and local partnerships.

Core Programs
Five Paths to a Fresh Start
From medical rehabilitation to a first paycheque, our programs meet people where they are and support them to where they want to be.

Rehabilitation, Assistive Access & MHPSS

Connecting survivors to medical care, assistive devices, physiotherapy, and trauma-informed mental health support so they can begin to heal.

Rehab-to-Work Pathways

Practical skills in phone repair, IT, tailoring, and agribusiness paired with job placement, starter kits, and mentorship to help people earn a living again.

Legal, Protection & Benefits Navigation

Helping people navigate pensions, entitlements, legal documentation, and representation so their rights are not just written down, but acted on.

Community Reintegration & Peacebuilding

Building peer networks, reducing stigma, and creating space for community dialogue so people can return home and belong.

Disability Inclusion in Humanitarian Response

Working with camps, local governments, and partner NGOs to ensure people with disabilities are not left out of emergency response and recovery.

Dignity & Rights

We uphold the inherent dignity and rights of every person with a disability, guided by international human rights standards.

Pilot Project
Inclusive Rehab-to-Work Pathway
A 3-month pilot in Abuja that brings rehabilitation, mental health support, skills training, and legal guidance together into one clear pathway for 40 people.
Month 1

Intake & Rehabilitation

  • Community outreach with welfare units, camp managers, and local leaders
  • Functional assessments and individualized rehab/MHPSS plans
  • Protection screening and baseline assessment
  • Safeguarding framework and accessible venue setup
Month 2

Skills Sprint & Support

  • Skills tracks: phone repair/IT, tailoring, or agribusiness
  • Life skills, disaster risk reduction, and conflict sensitivity
  • Legal/benefits navigation clinics
  • Ongoing psychosocial counselling and follow-up
Month 3

Placement & Reintegration

  • Starter toolkits and micro-grants for 20 participants
  • Employer referrals, cooperatives, and market linkage
  • Peer support groups for social reintegration
  • Endline assessment and partner learning review
40

Participants assessed, enrolled, and supported through the pathway

30+

Complete skills sprint with employable capabilities

50%+

Initiate income activities or secure placements within 30 days
