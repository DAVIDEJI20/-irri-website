What We Do

Our Programs

From medical care to a first paycheque, our programs meet people where they are and walk with them to where they want to be.

Program Areas
Five Pillars of Inclusive Recovery

IRRI's work is organized around five integrated program areas, each addressing a critical dimension of the rehabilitation and reintegration journey. From medical care and assistive devices to legal advocacy and economic empowerment, our programs are designed to work together, ensuring that beneficiaries receive comprehensive support that addresses the full spectrum of their needs. Every program is led by persons with disabilities and grounded in rights-based, evidence-driven, and community-centered approaches.

Our Work
Integrated Program Areas
Each program area is designed to reinforce the others, creating a comprehensive pathway from crisis to sustainable recovery.
Program 1

Medical Rehabilitation & Assistive Devices

When someone loses mobility or function through conflict or disaster, the first step is restoring what can be restored. We connect people to clinical rehabilitation, physiotherapy, prosthetics, and assistive devices, while providing the mental health support that makes physical recovery possible. Every plan is built around the individual, not the diagnosis.

Program 2

Economic Empowerment & Vocational Training

Recovery does not end at the clinic door. Our Rehab-to-Work pipeline turns rehabilitation outcomes into real livelihoods. Through market-aligned vocational training, digital skills, apprenticeships, and micro-enterprise support, people gain the tools and confidence to earn a living and participate in their local economies. Every training environment, material, and mentorship is designed to be fully inclusive.

Program 3

Legal Aid & Rights Advocacy

Rights are only as strong as people's ability to claim them. We help individuals navigate pensions, entitlements, legal documentation, and compensation claims so their protections are not just written down, but acted on. Where systemic barriers exist, we advocate for change alongside the people affected.

Program 4

Peacebuilding & Social Cohesion

Disability and conflict are deeply connected, yet persons with disabilities are routinely excluded from peace processes. IRRI creates space for inclusive dialogue, supports community reconciliation, and trains local peacebuilders to centre disability perspectives. By putting lived experience at the heart of peacebuilding, we help communities rebuild trust and reduce stigma.

Program 5

Humanitarian Response & Emergency Relief

In emergencies, persons with disabilities are among the most likely to be left behind. We work to make disability inclusion the standard across humanitarian response, from shelter and food distribution to health services and protection. We deploy rapid assessments, provide emergency assistive devices, and train responders on inclusive practices so that no one is overlooked when disaster strikes.

Pilot Project
3-Month Proof of Concept
Our structured pilot project demonstrates the impact of integrated programming across three carefully sequenced phases.
01

Assessment & Enrollment

Community outreach, disability screening, and needs assessment across pilot sites. This phase maps existing services, builds referral pathways, and establishes the baseline data that guides every decision that follows.

02

Rehabilitation & Skills Training

Participants receive individualised rehabilitation plans alongside market-aligned vocational training. Clinical care is combined with practical skills development and peer mentorship, so recovery and readiness move forward together.

03

Reintegration & Follow-up

The final phase focuses on what matters most: a life after the program. Livelihood placement, legal documentation, peer support groups, and structured follow-up ensure that progress made during the pilot translates into lasting change.
