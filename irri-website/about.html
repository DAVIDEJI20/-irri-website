Who We Are

Through Inclusive Reintegration

Across Nigeria, armed conflict, terrorism, and natural disasters leave thousands with newly acquired disabilities. For most, support ends when the emergency does.

People living with physical, sensory, and psychosocial disabilities face compounded barriers: disrupted services, limited healthcare, legal gaps, stigma, and exclusion from economic life. They are overlooked not because they lack potential, but because systems were not built with them in mind.

"We see a Nigeria where no one is defined by what happened to them. Where people who acquire disabilities through conflict or disaster are fully supported to rebuild their lives, on their own terms, in their own communities."

People in our programs gain access to rehabilitation, mental health support, and sustainable livelihood pathways. Everything we do is led by persons with disabilities and grounded in rights, evidence, and local partnerships.

Mission & Vision

What Drives Us Forward

Our Vision

A Nigeria where disability is never a barrier to belonging

We envision a society where people who acquire disabilities through conflict or disaster are fully supported to rebuild their lives with dignity, opportunity, and belonging. Where every individual has access to the support they need, and communities embrace inclusion as a strength.

Our Mission

Walking alongside people as they rebuild

Through rehabilitation, mental health and psychosocial support, skills training, and sustainable livelihood pathways. We work hand-in-hand with persons with disabilities, local communities, and institutional partners to create lasting change.

Our Values

The Principles That Guide Us

Seven core values rooted in human rights, lived experience, and a commitment to lasting change.

01

Dignity & Rights

We uphold the inherent dignity and rights of every person with a disability, guided by international human rights standards.

02

Nothing About Us Without Us

People with lived experience of disability and conflict lead our decisions, programs, and governance.

03

Inclusion in Action

Accessibility and participation are built into everything we do: programs, partnerships, and workplaces.

04

Do No Harm

We operate with conflict sensitivity, trauma-informed care, strong safeguarding, and protection-first principles.

05

Accountability & Integrity

We steward resources transparently, measure what matters, and are accountable to communities, partners, and donors.

06

Collaboration Over Silos

We work across humanitarian, development, security, and private-sector partners to deliver joined-up solutions.

07

Local Leadership

We prioritize Nigerian leadership, build local capacity, and design for scale and sustainability.

Rights-Based Approach

Grounded in International Standards

Our work is aligned with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) and international humanitarian law.

IRRI's work is built on a simple truth: disability rights are human rights. We draw on the CRPD's principles of non-discrimination, full participation, accessibility, and respect for inherent dignity.

Our approach recognises that disability, conflict, and displacement intersect in ways that multiply disadvantage. Every intervention we design is created with and for persons with disabilities, never imposed upon them.

We work to make disability inclusion the norm across humanitarian response, recovery planning, and development programming, not an afterthought.

UN CRPD Alignment

Programs designed around the Convention's core principles of dignity, autonomy, and full participation.

Humanitarian Standards

Guided by the IASC Guidelines on Inclusion of Persons with Disabilities in Humanitarian Action.

Do No Harm Principle

Conflict-sensitive programming with strong safeguarding and protection-first protocols.

Localization

Nigerian-led, community-driven approaches that build local capacity and ensure sustainability.
